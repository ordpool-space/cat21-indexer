About CAT-21

CAT-21 is a protocol that associates unique cat art with individual satoshis on Bitcoin. Every cat lives on a single sat — that sat becomes a CAT-21 ordinal, and ownership of the cat follows ownership of the sat. There is no off-chain database, no API key, no rendering server. The image is in the math.

Minting

A CAT-21 mint transaction is any Bitcoin transaction whose nLockTime is set to 21. No exceptions. The first sat of the first output receives the cat. Every mint reveals exactly one cat. The image and traits are derived from SHA256(transactionHash + blockHash) — a hash that no one can choose in advance.

Pay-to-taproot outputs (bc1p…) are recommended for best compatibility with Ordinals-aware wallets, but the protocol does not require it.

Consensus, not permission

Bitcoin is consensus. Ordinal Theory is consensus. CAT-21 is the same shape: if we agree that nLockTime=21 reveals a cat, it does. No authority enforces this. The protocol exists because its participants choose to follow it — the same mechanism that gives Bitcoin its value.

Cat identity

Each cat is unpredictable. The seed formula uses the block hash, which can only be known after the block is mined. No one chooses their cat. The cat was always there, waiting in the math — minting just reveals it.

Every Bitcoin transaction that does not set nLockTime=21 is a cat that will never be revealed. The choice to mint is the choice to reveal.

Scarcity is time

There is no supply cap. Anyone can mint a cat at any time. The protocol is permissionless and unstoppable. But scarcity here is not artificial — it is temporal. Cat #0 can never be minted again. Lower numbers can never be reassigned. Categories like sub1k, sub10k, sub100k emerge naturally as more cats are revealed. Every new mint makes every previous cat rarer. You are never late.

Proof of Cat Work

Bitcoin secures itself through accumulated proof of work. CAT-21 secures itself through accumulated mint fees. Every cat mint pays a fee to miners. Those fees, summed across every mint in history, are the Proof of Cat Work. The number only grows. It never weakens — not even during quiet periods. Each mint is one more entity declaring that the protocol matters.

The Genesis Cat

Cat #0 is the Genesis Cat. The first Bitcoin transaction in all of history to set nLockTime=21, minted at block height 824,205. It carries a price tag the protocol pins to it alone: one satoshi equals 21 BTC. The ultimate flip.

Whoever controls the Genesis Cat is the sole authority over protocol changes, future development, and the definition of the art. Governance updates are inscribed on the Genesis Cat's sat and become effective after 21 confirmations. The current holder has chosen the pixelated cat style you see across this site, and considers the cats perfect as they are.

Immutability

CAT-21 cats can't be destroyed. They are immutable on the Bitcoin blockchain. If a CAT-21 ordinal ever becomes unspendable, the cat still exists — it just no longer has an owner. The cat is free.
